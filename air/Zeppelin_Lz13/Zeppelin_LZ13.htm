The Zeppelin LZ 13 Hansa (or simply Hansa) was a German civilian rigid airship first flown in 1912 with a volume of 18,700 cubic metres.[1] It was first operated by DELAG to carry passengers and post and flew the first scheduled international passenger flights (starting with the visit to Denmark and Sweden in September 1912).[2] At the outbreak of World War I, the German military requisitioned it for use in attack flights, reconnaissance, and finally as a training airship.[3]

[edit] Civilian flights

The closed passenger cabin was attached to the hull behind the open control cabin and had room for 24 passengers. Count Zeppelin commanded the Hansa on the first commercial airship flight from to Denmark and Sweden in 1912-09-19);[2]

During two years of commercial DELAG service it carried 6,217 passengers on 399 flights,[3][4] covering 44,437 kilometres.[1][3]

[edit] Military use

  • The military added a platform to the top of the hull and installed two machine guns.[3]
  • attack missions on France and reconnaissance missions over the Baltic Sea (involved in the taking of Liepāja)[3]
  • From early 1915 used as a training airship, making over 500 flights over Berlin.[3]
  • Dismantled in August 1916.[3]
